Galaxy Clusters

Galaxy clusters are the result of the gravitational collapse of overdense regions that are seeded by processes in the early universe, followed by a sequence of mergers and accretion of surrounding material. These structures are important laboratories for investigations of structure formation and galaxy evolution, as well as useful cosmological probes. Through my work, I try to shed light on their history, their formation and how they fit within the larger cosmological framework. To understand the physics of these objects, I perform dynamical analyses using member galaxies as tracers of the clusters' gravitational potential.
